PRIVACY POLICY FOR NEUROHUE

Effective Date: November 4, 2024

INTRODUCTION

Welcome to NeuroHue ("we," "our," or "us"). We are committed to protecting your privacy and the privacy of children who use our application. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information and the information of children when you use our mobile application NeuroHue (the "App").

Please read this Privacy Policy carefully. By using the App, you agree to the practices described in this policy. If you do not agree with the practices described in this Privacy Policy, please do not use the App.

INFORMATION WE COLLECT

Personal Information

Depending on your role and use of the App, we may collect:

For Parents/Guardians:

  • Name, email address, phone number
  • Account login credentials
  • Relationship to child

For Children:

  • Name, age, gender
  • Developmental information
  • Activity performance data
  • Progress metrics

For Therapists/Healthcare Professionals:

  • Name, email address, phone number
  • Professional credentials
  • Employment information

For CDC/Facility Administrators:

  • Name, email address, phone number
  • Facility information

Usage Information

We automatically collect certain information about your device and how you interact with the App, including:

  • Device information (such as device type, operating system)
  • App usage statistics
  • Session duration and frequency
  • Features accessed
  • Performance data

Child Data Protection (COPPA Compliance)

We take children's privacy seriously and comply with the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA). We collect personal information from children under 13 only with verifiable parental consent. Parents have control over their child's information and can review, delete, or refuse further collection of their child's data.

HOW WE USE INFORMATION

We use the information we collect for the following purposes:

To Provide the App's Functionality:

  • Creating and managing user accounts
  • Enabling parent-child interaction features
  • Tracking developmental progress
  • Facilitating therapist-parent communication
  • Generating progress reports and analytics

To Improve and Develop the App:

  • Analyzing usage patterns to enhance user experience
  • Identifying and fixing technical issues
  • Developing new features based on user needs

To Communicate with You:

  • Responding to inquiries and support requests
  • Sending important updates about the App
  • Providing information about features relevant to your role

INFORMATION SHARING AND DISCLOSURE

We may share your information in the following circumstances:

Between Authorized Users: Information may be shared between parents/guardians, therapists, and facility administrators as necessary for the functioning of the App and only with appropriate permissions.

Service Providers: We may share information with third-party service providers who help us operate, develop, or improve the App. These providers are bound by confidentiality obligations.

Legal Requirements: We may disclose information if required by law, regulation, legal process, or governmental request.

Business Transfers: In the event of a merger, acquisition, or sale of all or part of our assets, user information may be transferred as part of the transaction.

We do not sell or rent personal information to third parties for marketing purposes.

DATA SECURITY

We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect the information we collect and maintain. However, no method of transmission or storage is 100% secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

Our security measures include:

  • Encryption of sensitive data
  • Secure database access controls
  • Regular security reviews
  • Employee training on data protection

DATA RETENTION

We retain personal information for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes outlined in this Privacy Policy, unless a longer retention period is required by law. When determining retention periods, we consider:

  • The amount, nature, and sensitivity of the information
  • The potential risk of harm from unauthorized use or disclosure
  • The purposes for which we process the information
  • Legal requirements

  • Access and review the information we have about you
  • Correct inaccurate or incomplete information

To exercise these rights, please contact us using the information provided in the "Contact Us" section.
